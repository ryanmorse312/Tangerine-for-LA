    Ryan Morse's Book Project

    Hello, this is my language arts project on the book "Tangerine" by Edward Bloor.

    This Book Was About a Boy Named Paul That is in Middle School

    His family moves a lot for one main reason, "The Erik Fisher Football Dream". Erik is his brother and his dad tries to get him the best oppurtunities he can to make it big in the world of sports. However, they don't know that there's a bad side of Erik. Paul sees that side very clearly.

    Erik is Dangerous

    He is a murderer, a theif, and many other things. Thankfully, at the end of the book, he and his friend, Arthur, are exposed at the Fishers' house during a gathering centered around the neighborhood roberies that they were responsible for.

    Tangerine County

    This is where Paul's family moved while following "The Erik Fisher Football Dream". It is a small town in florida that used to be dedicated to growing tangerines, but now the ecosystem has changed due mostly due the awful weather. It is almost always raining and storming which forces different living conditions. Very different from the town in Houston, Texas that he used to live in. The school he goes to is made mostly of small sheds for classrooms in case of a flood.

    The Muck Fire

    The definition of "Muck Fire" supplied by Google is; " A soil rich in carbon-based compounds from dead plants and organisms that stays burning for a long period of time. A muck fire ignites from the burning brush above and lightning strikes. Enough oxygen penetrates the parched, loosely packed peat, causing under ground embers to smolder for weeks." The muck fire in the story is in the Fishers' neighborhood and was seen as a bad thing. This acts as symbolism. It shows that this place or something in this place, like, say, Erik, is bad, and will always be bad.

    Soccer

    Erik may have Football, but Paul has Soccer. Soccer is Paul's favorite sport and shows that even in all of this bad, there can be good. Paul, despite being legally blind, is a very good goalkeeper. The fact that Paul's parents don't seem to notice that much but celebrate Erik's skill in Football also shows another bad thing in the book. I, personally, like this because all of the bad things in the book really made you pay attention and really brought emphasis to all of the good things that happened.

    Murder of Luis Death by Blackjack

    Luis was killed by Arthur using a blackjack. In the end of the book it is revealed that Arthur only commited the murder because Erik told him to.

    Neighborhood Robbery What's Your's is Mine

    The neighborhood the Fishers live in has been robbed. A meeting has been called at their house and Paul and Erik's mother reports that she has found some of the stolen items. It is then revealed that Erik and Arthur had stolen those items. The sherrif said that if they returned all that had been stolen then the people who had owned those items would not press charges. However, Erik and Arthur were later arrested.

    Death of Mike Costello Zap

    Mike Costello was introduced into the story at the beginning as a friendly character. He played for Erik's football team and it appeared that he wuld be holding the ball for Erik as Erik was the new starting kicker. Paul liked him and quickly befriended his brother. Soon after they had met, he was struck by lightning during a football practice. Paul later caught Erik and his friends laughing about his death in the backyard during a gathering for Mike.
